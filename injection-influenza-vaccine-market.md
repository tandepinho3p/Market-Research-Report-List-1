<p><p>The Injection Influenza Vaccine market includes two primary types: Trivalent and Quadrivalent vaccines. Trivalent Influenza Vaccines target three specific strains of the influenza virus, typically two A strains and one B strain. In contrast, Quadrivalent Influenza Vaccines are designed to protect against four strains, adding an additional B strain for broader protection. Both types are crucial for public health, helping to reduce the incidence of influenza and its related complications, particularly during seasonal outbreaks.</p></p>
