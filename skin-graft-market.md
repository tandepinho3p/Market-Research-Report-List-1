<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Skin Graft Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Autologous</li><li>Isogeneic</li><li>Allogeneic</li><li>Xenogeneic</li><li>Prosthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The skin graft market is categorized into five main types. Autologous grafts use the patient's skin, minimizing rejection risks. Isogeneic grafts involve skin from genetically identical individuals, such as twins. Allogeneic grafts use skin from different individuals of the same species, which may require immunosuppressive therapy. Xenogeneic grafts originate from different species and generally face higher rejection rates. Lastly, prosthetic skin substitutes are synthetic or bioengineered materials designed to mimic natural skin, offering alternatives for grafting.</p></p>
