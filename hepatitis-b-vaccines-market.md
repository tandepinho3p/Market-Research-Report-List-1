<p><p>The Hepatitis B vaccine market primarily consists of two types: yeast-derived and CHO (Chinese Hamster Ovary) cell-derived vaccines. Yeast-derived vaccines are produced using genetically engineered Saccharomyces cerevisiae, which facilitates the production of the hepatitis B surface antigen. CHO-derived vaccines, on the other hand, utilize Chinese Hamster Ovary cells for the same purpose, often providing higher yields and improved safety profiles. Both types are effective in generating an immune response against hepatitis B, contributing to global vaccination efforts.</p></p>
